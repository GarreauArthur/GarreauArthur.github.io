Artificial Intelligence is relatively old, but it really began to develop since the years 2010. The most successful area of AI is called Deep Learning, for it uses of deep neural networks. Deep Neural Networks achieve great performance on specific tasks such as object recognition, speech recognition or atari games. Yeah, some guys are so lazy, they trained DNN to play video games for them. But we don't know how to build models to excel at a wide variety of tasks. One of the most challenging goals, and I would say the ultimate goal of artificial intelligence is to build a general-purpose intelligence, an agent able to learn and perform any tasks.
We face several problems :
1. Catastrophic forgetting, DNN are really good at learning one task, but if the task change, they forget everything
1.1 solution ? Overcoming catastrophic forgetting in neural networks
1.2 deep Q-network was able to learn to play across a diverse range of 49 games.
2. Possibility to act

To be able to do any task, an intelligent agent needs a way to perform it, if it needs to move, it needs wheels or legs ... We could try to modify the environment so the agent could access to what he needs to perform tasks. But we want an agent able to interact with the world as it is now.
We don't model the world, we model the mind
